05 FAQ

Q

What should be the top priority when choosing a hotel in Hoi An Old Town?

A
“Access to the Old Town” is the most critical factor. The Old Town has long periods where vehicles are prohibited, so poor access can make getting around a major burden. It’s best to choose a hotel within easy walking distance to freely enjoy both the town’s bustle and its quiet moments.
Q

Where is the most convenient place to stay for visiting Cham Island?

A
Facilities on Cham Island are very limited, so we strongly recommend staying in Hoi An Old Town or An Bang Beach. Transfers to Cua Dai Port for speedboats can be easily arranged from major Hoi An hotels.
Q

What about small boutique hotels right in the center of the Old Town?

A
While they offer great atmosphere, their pools, breakfast, and service standards often fall short. This guide selects well-established “hotels” with stable service and solid facilities.
Q

Which hotels are best for runners?

A
“Bel Marina” and “RiverTown” are highly recommended, as they are directly connected to the riverside running course along the Thu Bon River. Running through the morning mist and feeling the river breeze is an exceptional experience.
Q

Is it okay to stay in Da Nang and visit Hoi An on a day trip?

A
The true charm of Hoi An lies in the evening after day-trippers leave and the quiet early morning before the town wakes up. Staying in Hoi An for at least 1–2 nights lets you feel the full shift in the town's mood and energy, and makes visiting Cham Island much smoother.
Q

What is the best time for sightseeing and running to avoid heat and crowds?

A
The “early morning (6:00–8:00)” is the absolute best time. The daytime in Hoi An is extremely hot, and it gets so crowded in the evening that walking becomes difficult. Running through the mist-shrouded Old Town, feeling the market’s energy, and returning for breakfast—this is the peak experience only possible when staying in Hoi An.
